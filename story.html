Capy The Author

    Capy the capybara lived a calm life by the warm rivers of 
Brazil, where the water moved slow and the sun felt kind. He liked it that way.
    One day, everything changed.
The “hind-leg creatures” came.
They moved fast, spoke loudly, and didn’t understand the 
quiet language of the river. Before Capy could run, they captured 
im and placed him in a metal cage. The world he knew faded behind him as the journey began.
It was long. Too long.
The air changed. The smells changed. 
    The sounds became unfamiliar. Capy didn’t know where he was 
going—but he knew he didn’t want it.
Eventually, they reached a new land: the USA.
Before Capy could be taken to the farm, a wealthy man noticed him. 
There was something different about Capy—his calm eyes, his stillness.
“I’ll take him,” the man said.
A deal was made. $1,000.
Capy had a new home.
At first, it was confusing. No river. No herd. Just quiet rooms 
and strange objects. But the man wasn’t like the others. He was patient.
He taught Capy something no capybara had ever learned.
Reading.
Writing.
At first, Capy didn’t understand the symbols. They looked like 
scratches on paper. But day by day, slowly, something clicked.
A year passed.
And one day, Capy picked up a pen… and began to write.
Not just words.
His story.
This story.
